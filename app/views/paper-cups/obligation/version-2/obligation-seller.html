Guidance Report disposable cups recycling data

If you sell drinks in disposable cups, you must report on the weight of cups you have sold, and the weight of cups collected for recycling. This is part of extended producer responsibility (EPR) regulations.

From: Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and Enviroment Agency

Published: Published 15 June 2022

Extended producer responsibility (EPR) for packaging regulations encourages organisations to recycle. This includes disposable cups. 

Check if you must report data

You’ll need to report data on certain kinds of disposable cups as part of EPR.

When you must create an account

You must create an account if:

  • your organisation fill drinks in disposable cups and sell them
  • your organisation sells these drinks from a UK premises
  • you have 10 or more people working full-time hours, or some part-time staff whose hours average out to be full-time in a year
For example, you employ a mix of full-time and part-time staff. Your part-time staff is made up of seasonal workers who only work a few hours each day. Their total hours add up to full time between the beginning of January and the end of December.

When you do not need to create an account

You do not need to create an account if you’re:

  • a registered charity
  • a franchisee - your organisation as a whole will report data for selling cups

You’ll use a new online service to report and submit this data twice every year.

What disposable cups apply

You must report data on fibre-based composite cups. These cups are a type of single-use disposable cup with a waterproof lining. They are used to sell things like takeaway coffee.

You do not need to report data on other disposable cups, such as:

  • bamboo cups
  • cups that are only made of plastic
  • cups that are only made of paper
You do not need to report data on lids, cups sleeves and stirrers

What you need to do if you sell drinks in disposable cups

By 30 September 2024, you must be registered so that you can:

  • install a recycling bin to collect the cups 
  • record the weight of cups in kilograms that are sold and cups that are collected 
  • make arrangements for the used cups to be collected and recycled 
  • submit the data to the appropriate environmental regulator for every nation your organisation operates in

How much it costs

You must pay £141 per year to meet your requirements to collect and report disposable cups. You must make this payment to the environmental regulator in the nation where your organisation is registered.

Installing a recycling bin

You’ll need a dedicated recycling bin for disposable cups. It should only contain empty disposable cups.

There should be no additional waste, such as:

  • lids, cups sleeves and stirrers
  • liquids
  • other waste packaging

 Your recycling bin must be visible to the public and easily accessible.

Get help reporting data: using a takeback scheme

You may want to join a takeback scheme to help you meet your requirements for recycling disposable cups.

Businesses called takeback schemes can:

  • check if you need to report data on disposable cups
  • collect the cups from your premises
  • weigh the cups
  • arrange the recycling
  • report the data
  • submit the data to the regulator

Takeback schemes will report data on all their members and their premises in one go.

What data you need to collect and report

You'll have to collect and report the weight of cups that you’ve: 

  • sold
  • sent for recycling 
If you sell cups in more than one nation in the UK, you’ll have to give the weight sold in each nation.

You must report twice a year using the online service.

Keep your records in kilograms. You must keep data records for 7 years.

When you can report your cups data

These dates are for collecting and submitting data in 2024 and 2025:

  • First data submission period is from 1 October 2024 to 30 June 2025
  • First data submission period is from 1 July 2025 to 31 October 2025

Sign in or create an account

Sign in or create an account to register and report disposable cups recycling data

Before you create an account

There are a number of things you will need to have ready before you create an account.

You’ll need your:

  • companies house number
  • role within the organisation
  • organisation address
  • trading name
  • UK telephone number

You’ll also need to confirm what your organisation is registered as.
