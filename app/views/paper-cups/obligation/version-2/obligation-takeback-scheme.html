Guidance Report takeaway cups data for takeback scheme members

As a takeback scheme, you can report data for organisations who sell drinks in takeaway cups and support them with their responsibilities.

This is part of the extended producer responsibility (EPR) for packaging regulations, which encourages organisations to recycle.

From: Department for Environment, Food & Rural Affairs and Enviroment Agency

Published: Published 15 June 2022

Organisations who sell drinks in takeaway cups can ask you to report data on the cups that they recycle.

To support these organisations with their cup recycling, you’ll need to:

  • get approved and register as a takeback scheme
  • pay yearly registration fees
  • setup an account to use the online service to report data

What you need to do as a takeback scheme

You can help organisations selling drinks in takeaway cups to meet their requirements to report data on cups sold and cups recycled.

These organisations will be members of your takeback scheme.

As a takeback scheme you can support any members you have to:

  • check if they need to report data on takeaway cups
  • collect the cups from your premises
  • weigh the cups
  • arrange the recycling
  • report the data
  • submit the data to the regulator

You can report data on all your members and their premises in one go.

You’ll use the new online service to submit this data.

Reporting data on takeaway cups

You will need to use the online service to report 2 pieces of data twice per year:

  • the weight of cups that your member has sold
  • the weight of cups that your member has sent for recycling

How much it costs to be a takeback scheme

As a takeback scheme you must pay a registration fee of £3,726 every year to each individual nation your members operate in.

You must also pay £74 for each member you work with. This fee must be paid individually to each nation your member operates in.

Organisations you might work with

Your members will be organisations for example:

  • cafes
  • coffee shops
  • airlines
  • leisure centres
  • transport hubs like stations and airports

Types of cups that apply

You need to report data for your members on fibre-based composite cups. These cups are a type of single-use takeaway cup with a waterproof lining. They are used to sell things like takeaway coffee.

You do not need to report data for your members on other takeaway cups, such as:

  • bamboo cups
  • cups that are only plastic
  • cups that are only paper

You’ll use the new online service to submit this data.

How you can support your members with takeaway cups

You can provide additional support to help your members:

  • arrange collection of the takeaway cups in their recycling bins.
  • get the weight of cups they have gathered for recycling.

Approval process to become a takeback scheme

You need to follow the approval process to become a takeback scheme

1. Identify which nation you’ll register with

You will need to register to become a takeback scheme to help organisations report data on takeaway cups.

You must apply to the UK nation where your registered office is based. For example, if your registered office is based in London, you only need to apply to be a takeback scheme in England.

2. Contact the environmental regulator for your nation

In England

  • Environmental Agency (EA)
  • enquiries@environment-agency.gov.uk
  • Telephone: 03708 506 506

In Scotland

  • Scottish Environment Protection Agency (SEPA)
  • enquiries@environment-agency.gov.uk
  • Telephone: 03708 506 506

In Wales

  • Natural Resources Wales
  • enquiries@naturalresourceswales.gov.uk
  • Telephone: 03000 996 699

In Northern Ireland

  • Department of Agriculture, Environment and Rural Affairs (DAERA)
  • daera.helpline@daera-ni.gov.uk
  • Telephone: 0300 200 7850

3. Getting your application reviewed

The environmental regulator will review your application and tell you if you are approved.

If you are approved the environmental regulator will email you with an approval code.

If you lose this approval code you will need to contact the same environmental regulator to request a new one.

Create an account or sign in

Create an account or sign in to report takeaway cups recycling data for your members.

Before you create an account

To create an account you’ll need an email address and password. Then you’ll also need your:

  • approval code (this was emailed to you when you applied to be a takeback scheme)
  • companies house number
  • role within the organisation
  • organisation address
  • trading name
  • UK telephone number

You’ll also need to confirm what your organisation is registered as.

After you create an account

After you create an account, you’ll be able to:

  • register your members in every nation where they have premises once per year
  • submit cups data on behalf of your members twice per year

You must keep any data you collect for 7 years.
