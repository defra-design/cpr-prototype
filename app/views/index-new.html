Producer readiness Service homepage / dashboard

Introduction
The initial Alpha prototype work was based around users registering in year one of the EPR for Packaging scheme. As more details about timelines emerged, it became clear that users may have to complete actions across a number of obligation years at the same time.

This also coincided with evidence from our prototype testing that users who are obligated as both a 'producer' and 'seller' didn't comprehend the distinction between the two sets of reporting data. This could result in inaccurate data being added to the service. We believed part of this issue was related to the use of the 'task list' pattern, as it wasn't giving users the clarity they needed.

Our design exploration focussed on resolving these two issues. We mapped out the proposed timelines on MIRO then worked on a few design concepts before reviewing them with the team. We then created a lo-fi prototype of the v1 design iteration in Sketch.

Extended Producer Responsibility for Packaging (EPR-P) End-to-end prototypes

Introduction
When starting Alpha the EPR scheme was still in consultation, and there were a number of proposals around how the eventual service model could work.

Our service designer mapped out these different service models, scoring them using Lou Downe's good service scale, to understand any risk levels.

To test the service models with users, we created lo-fi clickable prototypes using Sketch, starting with what we considered to be the riskiest model.

We went through a number of rounds of testing of these service models, sharing the feedback with policy to help inform their decisions.

Deposit Return Scheme (DRS) Alpha prototypes

Introduction
Deposit Return Scheme (DRS) is another service that will sit under the Collection & Packaging Reforms programme. DRS is expected to launch after EPR-P, but whilst in Alpha we wanted to do some early exploration of the users and requirements of this service.

Working alongside our business analyst and the policy team, we developed some early prototypes based on the business requirements, and utilised the core functionality that had been developed for the EPR-P designs.

No testing has taken place on the DRS designs.
